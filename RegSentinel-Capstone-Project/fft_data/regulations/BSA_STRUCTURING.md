# Prohibition on Structuring

**Citation:** 31 USC § 5324 / 31 CFR § 1010.314  
**Authority:** FinCEN — Bank Secrecy Act  

## Summary

It is illegal to structure transactions to evade the CTR filing requirement.

## Key Requirements

- No person may structure, assist in structuring, or attempt to structure any transaction to evade CTR reporting.
- Detection of structuring triggers a SAR filing obligation.
- Applies regardless of whether the underlying funds are legitimate.

## Detection Indicators

- Multiple cash deposits just under $10,000 (e.g., $9,000–$9,900) on consecutive days.
- Same customer depositing sub-threshold amounts at multiple branches the same day (smurfing).
- Cash transactions deliberately broken into smaller amounts.

## Enforcement

Criminal penalty: up to 5 years imprisonment; up to 10 years if part of another crime or a pattern exceeding $100,000/year.
